Tank & Tankless Water Heater Installation & Repair

Traditional tank water heaters remain a dependable and cost-effective solution for many homes. Whether you need a quick replacement or a full system upgrade, we provide professional tank water heater installation and repair with a focus on safety, efficiency, and long-term reliability — to current California code.

Tankless water heaters provide on-demand hot water while improving energy efficiency and saving space. We specialize in tankless installation, repair, and system upgrades, ensuring proper sizing, gas supply, and venting for optimal performance.

Our services include

  • Tank water heater installation and replacement
  • Gas and electric system service
  • Leak repair and component replacement
  • Expansion tank and safety upgrades
  • Code corrections and permit-ready installations
  • Tankless water heater installation and replacement
  • System descaling and maintenance
  • Troubleshooting and repair
  • Gas line upgrades and venting configuration
  • Conversion from tank to tankless systems

Clean Installation, Consistent Performance

We take a detailed approach to sizing, venting, and system setup to ensure your water heater delivers consistent hot water without unnecessary strain on the system. Whether it's a straightforward replacement or a more involved upgrade, our work is done cleanly and correctly the first time. Tankless systems in particular require proper design and installation to perform — we make sure yours is configured to meet your home's hot water demand without fluctuation or performance issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Tank heaters cost less upfront and suit steady, predictable hot-water use. Tankless costs more to install but gives endless on-demand hot water, saves space, and lasts longer. We size both options to your household and budget and give you a straight recommendation.

  • A traditional tank typically lasts 8–12 years; tankless units often 15–20 with regular descaling. If yours is aging, leaking, or running out of hot water, we'll advise repair vs. replacement honestly.
